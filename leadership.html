Teaching Assistantships

During my time at the UW, I served as a teaching assistant in Solid and Gel Mechanics and Introduction to Engineering Design courses. Through teaching, not only have I gained further understanding of the topics technically, I have also learned to explain concepts in ways students can understand rather than how I prefer to approach. More importantly, I have been fortunate to have incredible mentors who have helped shaped my professional career, and I am grateful to be able to pay it forward through teaching the next generation of engineers.



Bioengineers Without Borders

Taking on one of the Grand Challenges in Global Health launched by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, I co-founded Anophelseize, looking to develop a low-cost capturing device for male mosquitoes of the species Anopheles gambiae to be used for field research. As part of UW BWB and in collaboration with the Target Malaria Research Consortium and the Uganda Virus Research Institute, I led an interdisciplinary team of 18 undergraduate and graduate students in this effort. I helped secure $2,000+ in prototype funding and exceled in health innovation competitions.



Undergraduate Research Program

Research has defined my undergraduate experience and I am constantly looking to encourage students to find their passions through research. To do so, I served as an Undergraduate Research Leader and a symposium abstract reviewer with UW’s Undergraduate Research Program. Through the program, I have been able to tell my story in first year classrooms and UW Daily interviews, thereby providing inspiration and logistical support for students looking to perform undergraduate research in all disciplines at the UW.



Associated Students of the University of Washington

Outside of the classroom, I am passionate about promoting diversity and ensuring that every student at the UW has the opportunity to achieve their goals. Working within the ASUW, I developed as a Leader in the UW Leaders program, served on the Personnel Committee to help students take on leadership roles, campaigned for Board of Directors that share my same vision, and helped first year students transition to the UW as a Dawg Daze Leader. I am committed to continue fostering diverse communities to achieve our common goals. To illustrate it quite simply, people are my passion.



STEM Outreach

Reflecting on my young scientific career, the biggest lesson I have learned is that if I really aspire to serve the global community, I cannot do it alone. By integrating my experiences with my leadership style, I constantly look to mentor the next generation of talented difference-makers in carving their own paths. From inspiring future scientists serving as an advisor for the UW School of Medicine to sharing my passion for bioengineering at the annual Engineering Discovery Days, I continue to develop my capacity for leadership, setting the foundation to become a life-long community builder and teacher.
